# Your Questions for Recruiter: GitLab Commercial AE Mid-Market

## Priority Questions (Ask 3-4 of these)

### 1. "How is the mid-market East territory structured? Is it geographic, named accounts, or a hybrid?"
**Why this works:** Shows you think about territory management strategically, not just "give me accounts." Also helps you understand if this is a hunting role, farming role, or both.

### 2. "With the Duo Agent Platform going GA in January, how is that changing the sales conversation in mid-market? Are reps leading with AI or is it still platform-first?"
**Why this works:** Demonstrates you've researched the latest product launch and are thinking about how to position it. Also signals you follow the company's news.

### 3. "What does quota attainment look like across the mid-market team right now? What percentage of reps are hitting plan?"
**Why this works:** This is a sophisticated question that tells you whether the territory plan is realistic. If fewer than 50% of reps are hitting quota, that's a signal about territory design or product-market fit challenges.

### 4. "Can you walk me through the interview process and timeline? Who would I be meeting with?"
**Why this works:** Standard but essential. Helps you prepare for each stage and understand the decision-making structure.

## Backup Questions (If conversation flows well)

### 5. "How does the new logo team work with the named account team? When a new logo closes, what does the handoff look like?"
**Why this works:** Shows you understand GitLab's sales org structure (from the handbook) and are thinking about post-sale adoption - which the JD emphasizes.

### 6. "What does GitLab look for in top-performing mid-market AEs? Is there a common profile or trait that separates the top 20% from the rest?"
**Why this works:** Positions you as someone who wants to be a top performer and is willing to learn what it takes. Also gives you intel on what to emphasize in later interviews.

### 7. "How does the team collaborate day-to-day in an all-remote environment? Is it mostly async through GitLab and Slack, or are there regular syncs?"
**Why this works:** Shows you're thoughtful about remote work culture and are evaluating whether the communication style fits how you work.

## Questions to AVOID with the Recruiter

- Don't ask about base salary negotiation (too early)
- Don't ask about PTO or benefits in detail (signals you're thinking about time off, not work)
- Don't ask questions you could easily google (shows lack of preparation)
- Don't ask about the stock price decline (saves this for later if relevant)

## Closing Statement

"Based on what I've learned about the role and the company, I'm genuinely excited about this opportunity. My experience selling AI-powered platforms with MEDDPICC, combined with my mid-market track record, feels like a strong fit. What's the best next step from here?"
