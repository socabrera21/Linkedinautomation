## Gaps & Mitigation

### Gap: No direct DevSecOps / developer tools sales experience
**Reality check:** This is a "Medium" gap, not a critical one. The JD says "ideally including development or DevSecOps teams" - it's a preference, not a hard requirement.
**Mitigation strategy:**
- Lean into Rapid7 experience (cybersecurity/vulnerability management is half of DevSecOps)
- Highlight that you've sold to IT Directors and CISOs at every role - these are GitLab buyers
- Show genuine interest: mention you've explored GitLab's platform, understand the CI/CD pipeline concept, and see the parallel to how you've sold platform consolidation before (Aisera replacing multiple IT tools)
**Talking point:** "While I haven't sold directly to developer teams, my experience at Rapid7 selling security solutions to InfoSec and IT leaders maps directly to the 'Sec' in DevSecOps. At Agiloft, I sold an AI platform that consolidated multiple tools into one - which is exactly GitLab's value proposition. I also understand the technical evaluation process, having managed POCs, integrations, and security reviews in every role."

### Gap: GitLab-specific product knowledge
**Mitigation:** This is expected for any external hire. Show you've done homework:
- Reference Duo Agent Platform (just went GA in January)
- Mention the "AI Paradox" (only 20% of dev time is coding)
- Reference GitLab 18.0 packaging changes (Duo included in Premium)
- Know the Premium vs. Ultimate tier structure

### Gap: Open source community experience
**Mitigation:** Frame as a learning opportunity tied to GitLab's Transparency value. "I'm drawn to GitLab's open-core model and the transparency it represents. While I'm newer to open source, I see the same principles in how GitLab operates - the public handbook, the public issue tracker. I'm excited to learn from that community."
