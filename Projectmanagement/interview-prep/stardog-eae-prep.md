### 7. Objection Handling Preparation

#### Common Enterprise Objections:

**"We're already working with [Competitor]"**
Response framework:
- Acknowledge and validate
- Ask discovery questions about current gaps/pain points
- Differentiate based on specific capabilities
- Position as complementary or superior alternative

**"The price is too high"**
Response framework:
- Reframe around value/ROI, not cost
- Break down TCO vs. current state
- Quantify business impact metrics
- Offer flexible commercial models (pilots, phased approaches)

**"We need to see this work in our environment"**
Response framework:
- Agree and propose structured POC
- Define clear success criteria upfront
- Limit scope to prove specific use case
- Include executive sponsorship in POC design

**"This seems too complex/risky to implement"**
Response framework:
- Share similar customer success stories
- Outline phased implementation approach
- Highlight professional services support
- Reference user community and ecosystem

### 9. Red Flags to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Badmouth previous employers or customers
- Focus solely on individual achievement (show team collaboration)
- Demonstrate arrogance or overconfidence
- Fail to ask thoughtful questions
- Show unfamiliarity with Stardog or the space
- Overemphasize feature/function over business value
- Lack specific metrics and outcomes in examples

**Do:**
- Show genuine curiosity about the product and market
- Demonstrate strategic thinking, not just tactical execution
- Balance confidence with humility
- Provide specific, quantifiable examples
- Show adaptability and learning orientation
- Emphasize customer-centricity
- Display executive presence and communication skills
